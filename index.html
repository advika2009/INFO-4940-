Interactive web piece 2026 Final exhibition project

Emotion Mismatch Music Interface

A music-based interactive website that asks users what emotion they feel and then generates a deliberately mismatched daily mix. The piece plays with expectation, platform familiarity, and emotional dissonance by pairing a reported feeling with songs and feedback that do not comfortably match it.

Concept

This project explores how digital systems can misread, reshape, or manipulate emotion. Instead of comforting the user, the interface reflects back an uncanny version of emotional personalization.

Webcam-based interactive experiment 2026 Concept + prototype

Hand Tracking / Gesture Distortion Study

An experimental webcam piece built around tracking hand coordinates and turning movement into a system response. The project investigates discomfort, labeling, and the unstable relationship between what the body does and what a digital system claims it sees.

Concept

The core idea is that computational interpretation is not neutral. A gesture can be delayed, reframed, or falsely labeled, making the viewer aware of how systems assign meaning to behavior.

Playful browser interaction 2026 Earlier class project

Cursor Bubble World

A browser-based project where the screen responds to the cursor and emits colorful bubbles in motion. The piece focuses on immediacy, responsiveness, and delight while testing how simple interactions can still feel alive and memorable.

Concept

This work is less about critique and more about motion, feedback, and visual reward. It helped develop a stronger sense of user interaction and screen behavior through lightweight real-time response.

Speculative media critique 2026 Video-based class project

Spotify X Concept Video

A conceptual media project that used strong voice, pacing, and platform language to critique recommendation systems and digital incentives. The piece started as a stylized intervention and later pushed toward making the critique more explicit for the viewer.

Concept

The project examines the hidden logic behind platforms: what they optimize for, what they encourage, and how users are subtly shaped by system goals they rarely see.
